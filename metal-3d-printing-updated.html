Metal 3D Printing (Metal-Infused Filament Workflow)

Completed

Summary: Designed and documented a complete BMD metal printing workflow using metal-infused filament. Addressed filament brittleness with a custom spool holder and filament warmer, implemented purge procedures to prevent nozzle clogging, programmed debinding and sintering cycles, and characterized shrinkage to support accurate scaling.

Overview

Bound Metal Deposition uses plastic-bound metal powder to produce a green part that must be debound and sintered to become fully metallic. This project focused on making that entire workflow reliable enough for a shared shop environment rather than treating the printer as a magical goblin box that spits out miracles.

I developed printer setup procedures, spool-feed modifications, purge routines, and furnace programs so the process could be repeated by other users with less trial-and-error. The final result was a documented workflow covering printing, post-processing, and troubleshooting from start to finish.

Technical approach

The first challenge was filament brittleness. Standard side-mounted feeding introduced enough bending to snap the filament, so I used a vertical spool feed and a filament warmer mounted close to the print head. The second challenge was clogging: if metal filament sat in the nozzle without active extrusion, the binder could burn away and leave fused powder behind. To avoid that, I built purge steps into the workflow before and immediately after printing.

After printing, the parts move through debinding and sintering cycles with dedicated furnace programs. I also tracked dimensional change through the process to support shrinkage compensation when preparing CAD geometry.

Key contributions

  • Created a vertical spool holder and warmer setup to reduce filament breakage during feeding.
  • Developed purge and startup procedures to minimize nozzle clogging from burned binder and fused powder.
  • Documented printer calibration, print setup, shutdown, debinding, and sintering workflows for shop use.
  • Defined furnace programs for debinding and sintering steps.
  • Characterized shrinkage behavior to inform part scaling and dimensional expectations.
