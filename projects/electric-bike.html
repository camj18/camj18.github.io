Electric Bike (DIY Pedal-Assist System)

Status: Incomplete

Summary: Designed and partially built a custom assist system with emphasis on safe high-power wiring, mechanical integration, and realistic torque and speed constraints.

Overview

The intent was to force real electromechanical design decisions: drivetrain behavior, mounting stiffness, wiring safety, and serviceability. The target was predictable torque-focused assist rather than peak speed.

The project was interrupted before full system testing, but it includes meaningful design work in architecture, bracket fabrication, and high-current safety practices.

Tools and skills

Mechanical design High-power electronics Wiring Fabrication
Expand details architecture • mount • next iteration

Design / technical approach

  • Electrical system: high-current controller + fused critical connections to limit fault cascades.
  • Mechanical integration: rigid motor bracket fabricated using waterjet and manual mill.
  • Drivetrain planning: gear ratios selected to bias torque assist while controlling top speed.
  • Freewheel strategy: dual freewheels to prevent back-driving between pedals and motor.

What I learned / would improve

For a future version, I would start with a complete schematic and harness plan, define an enclosure strategy, and design mounting around serviceability and theft resistance from day one.
