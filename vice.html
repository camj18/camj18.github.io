VICE: Vibram Impact Cushioning Evaluator

Status: Prototype 1

Summary: Designed and prototyped a pendulum-based test rig to quantify outsole energy return using repeatable impact physics and encoder-based motion measurement.

Overview

The rig delivers a controlled impact, defines input energy by release angle, and infers returned energy from rebound angle. The design focuses on stiffness, repeatability, and measurement credibility.

Tools and skills

CAD Sensors/encoder Test rig design MATLAB
Expand details mechanical • instrumentation • next iteration

Key mechanical features

  • 80/20 frame with reinforcement plates for stiffness
  • Dual-plate pendulum arm to resist torsion
  • Bearing-supported axle to reduce friction variability
  • Latch + release designed for repeatable launch conditions

Instrumentation & data pipeline

A rotary encoder on the axle streams angle data to an Arduino, then into analysis to compute energy-in, energy-out, and percent return per impact.

Next iteration priorities

Tighter alignment controls, a more robust latch, and a documented calibration routine with uncertainty reporting.
